Social Impact

My collaborations have garnered nearly a quarter of a million dollars in major public and private grants to expand legal representation and build power in impacted communities. I have domain expertise in grant writing, project management, policy analysis, and program evaluation. I am frequently invited to give talks and present my research to academic and non-academic audiences including government, civil legal aid, and community-based organizations.

Mariposa Legal: Collective organization focused on providing legal representation to people in Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) custody in Indiana. The National Qualified Representative Program: Federal program that provides appointed zealous, independent, person-centered legal representation for individuals who have been found incompetent to represent themselves in immigration proceedings because of serious mental health or cognitive health disabilities.

The Midwest Immigration Bond Fund: A revolving fund that posts bonds for individuals held in ICE custody in Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, and Wisconsin and provides comprehensive post-release support, mutual aid, and community care for impacted people and families.

Communities Not Cages: An archive documenting the fight against the Clay County jail expansion for ICE in Indiana, de-carcerating across the urban-rural divide. The fight began after Illinois ended its ICE contracts and ICE sought bed space in neighboring Indiana in August 2021. Despite federal and state policy reversals, Clay moved forward with the jail expansion on March 2022. A federal lawsuit, Herrera Cardenas et al. v. ICE et al., exposing inhumane conditions was filed April 2022. The fight continues.
